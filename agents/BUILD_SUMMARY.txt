AGENT GROUP RATIONALE
---------------------
Five agents designed around the distinct business personas who consume loyalty 
program data at an auto parts retailer. Each agent owns a clear scope with 
defined view access, guardrails, and cross-agent referrals for out-of-scope 
questions. All agents query the same RewardsLoyaltyData semantic model.

Design principles:
  • One agent per business function — no overlap in primary responsibility
  • Every semantic view is covered by at least one agent as primary
  • Cross-agent referrals create a connected experience (no dead ends)
  • Guardrails are consistent: no PII in aggregates, no fabrication, no predictions
  • Examples use realistic auto parts retail numbers and terminology

VERIFIED ANSWERS (15 total — 3 per agent)
------------------------------------------
Verified answers are a Power BI semantic model feature ("Prep data for AI").
Each is a report visual tied to trigger phrases — when a user asks a matching
question, the pre-built visual is returned instead of a generated response.

Deployment: Create report visuals matching each answer → right-click visual →
"Set up a verified answer" → add the question text as trigger phrases.

Files per agent:
  verified-answers-loyalty.json   (Loyalty Program Manager)
  verified-answers-store-ops.json (Store Operations)
  verified-answers-merch.json     (Merchandising)
  verified-answers-marketing.json (Marketing & Promotions)
  verified-answers-csr.json       (Customer Service)

1. LOYALTY PROGRAM MANAGER (3 verified answers)

   Q: "What can you help me with?"
   A: Scope overview — tier distribution, enrollment trends, churn risk, points
      liability, engagement health, member lookups, and cross-agent referrals.

   Q: "How are the membership tiers structured?"
   A: Four-tier breakdown (Bronze ~60%, Silver ~25%, Gold ~10%, Platinum ~5%)
      with descriptions, target distributions, and tier movement rules.

   Q: "What counts as a churned member?"
   A: Risk classification by days since last purchase — Active (0–90), Watch
      (91–180), At Risk (181–365), Lapsed (365+). Prioritizes Gold/Platinum.

2. STORE OPERATIONS (3 verified answers)

   Q: "What can you help me with?"
   A: Scope overview — store revenue rankings, regional comparisons, transaction
      volumes, return rates, channel mix, and member penetration by store.

   Q: "What store types does AAP have?"
   A: Two types — Hub (full-service, regional anchors) and Satellite (smaller
      footprint, extending coverage). Both serve all three channels.

   Q: "What channels are tracked for transactions?"
   A: Three channels — In-Store, Online, and Phone. Every transaction is tagged
      with channel for revenue and volume breakdowns at any level.

3. MERCHANDISING & CATEGORY MANAGER (3 verified answers)

   Q: "What can you help me with?"
   A: Scope overview — category performance, brand analysis, SKU metrics, return
      rates, bonus-eligible products, revenue concentration. Notes: revenue only,
      no cost/margin data.

   Q: "What product categories does AAP carry?"
   A: 10 categories — Batteries, Oil & Fluids, Brakes, Filters, Wipers, Spark
      Plugs, Lighting, Coolant, Accessories, Electrical. With examples for each.

   Q: "What does bonus-eligible mean for a product?"
   A: SKU flag indicating extra bonus points earned on top of base rate. Explains
      standard vs. bonus earning, skip_sku flag, and merchandising implications.

4. MARKETING & PROMOTIONS (3 verified answers)

   Q: "What can you help me with?"
   A: Scope overview — campaign performance, coupon funnels, tier targeting,
      discount type comparison, coupon liability, campaign comparisons.

   Q: "How is coupon redemption rate calculated?"
   A: Formula (Redeemed ÷ Total Issued × 100) plus full coupon lifecycle status
      definitions (Issued, Redeemed, Expired, Voided) and reporting conventions.

   Q: "What types of coupon campaigns does AAP run?"
   A: Named campaigns (Holiday Blitz, Spring Tune-Up, Premium Member Exclusive,
      Welcome Offer), discount types (Percent Off, Fixed Dollar), and tier
      targeting mechanics.

5. CUSTOMER SERVICE & SUPPORT (3 verified answers)

   Q: "What can you help me with?"
   A: Scope overview — CSR agent activity, member lookups, audit trail, activity
      breakdowns, department analysis, service volume trends.

   Q: "What CSR activity types are tracked?"
   A: Six types — Member Lookup, Points Adjustment, Coupon Void, Tier Inquiry,
      Account Update, Escalation. Each with description and record fields.

   Q: "What departments handle customer service?"
   A: Four departments — General Support, Loyalty Services, Promotions Support,
      Escalations. With primary responsibilities and available report types.
